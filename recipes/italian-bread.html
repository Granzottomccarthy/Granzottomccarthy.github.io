Great Italian Bread

From the kitchen of: James Granzotto

Prep: 3 hours

Number of servings: 2 or 3 loaves or 15 hard rolls

INSTRUCTIONS:

Stir yeast in tepid water and sugar.

Set aside 5 minutes, until foamy.

Stir cold water into foamy yeast mixture.

Mix flour and salt together.

Use mixer at high speed, blend two cups of flour and yeast for 3 minutes.

Add remaining flour, mixing with spoon---rapidly.

When dough forms a rough like ball, cover and let rest 5 minutes.Hand kneed 50 strokes.

Let rest again 2 minutes and knead 20 strokes.

Cover and allow dough to rise in warm place for 1 hour.

Pat dough to a 16 inch long rectangle. Fold it in three. Repeat.

Return dough to bowl and let rise 1 1/2 to 2 hours to triple in bulk.

This dough makes one of the following: 2 or 3 long fat loaves---3 round fat loaves---15 hard rolls---6 cornetti (star shaped loaves)

Directions for shaping:

Pat dough to a rectangle. Square all corners. Fold dough lengthwise.

Using the heel of your hand press and pound the dough firmly along seams to seal all edges. A firm hand here reactivates the yeast to give your loaf more volume.

With seam on top, repeat the previous step.

Divide length of dough for above selected shapes.

Cover and let rest 5 minutes.

With the side of your hand and seam up, hand press a trench lengthwise.

Fold dough lengthwise.

Press and pound all edges together.

Rotate dough rapidly under your palms.

Start at middle and move to the ends.

Repeat step to extend length of dough for choice of loaves selected.

With seam up, place each shape on a baking tile sprinkled with corn meal.

Cover and let rise for 1 1/2 hours.

Before baking, slash top of loaves with sharp razor. Hold razor almost flat.

Directions for baking:

Oven temperature, 450 degrees.

Baking times:

Large loaves, 20 to 25 minutes then 10 min. at 400.

Small loaves, 15 to 20 min. plus 10 min. in a turned off oven.
