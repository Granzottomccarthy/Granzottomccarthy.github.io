INSTRUCTIONS:

Place the artichoke hearts, cheese, mayo, garlic, and basil in a blender or food processor and puree until fairly smooth.

Place breast between 2 sheet of waxed paper and pound with a mallet to a uniform thickness of about 1/8 inch.

Season to taste with salt and pepper.

Top each breast with a slice of prosciutto cut to fit the shape of the breast.

Spread prosciutto with a thin layer of the artichoke mixture.

Roll up jelly-roll style and secure lengthwise with a skewer or toothpicks, or tie crosswise in several places with cotton string or unwaxed dental floss.

Preheat oven to 350 F

Heat the olive oil and butter in an ovenproof sauté pan or skillet over med heat(I use a cast iron skillet and it works great).

Add chicken rolls and cook, turning frequently until lightly browned, about 5 min.

Transfer the pan to the oven and cook 10 min. Remove the chicken from the oven and let stand until just cool enough to handle

Remove the skewers or string.

Slice horizontally on a slight diagonal into 1/3-inch-wide pieces.

Serve warm or at room temperature, with sauce on the side if desired.


*NOTE: You will need approximately 12 very small artichokes or 4 large ones for this dish.

To cook fresh artichoke hearts, cut off the stem and the top 1/3 of each artichoke, and remove the course outer leaves.

As each heart is prepared, place it in water to which a bit of fresh lemon juice or vinegar has been added(to prevent discoloration).

To cook, drain artichokes, place in pan, add water to cover.

Bring to boil and boil until tender when pierced, 20-25 min.

Alternatively, steam over boiling water until tender.

Drain, and when cool enough to handle, remove leaves and cut away and discard fuzzy choke.

Chop just the hearts for this recipe. Reserve the leaves for another use.
