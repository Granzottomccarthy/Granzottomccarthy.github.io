INSTRUCTIONS:

In a 4 quart pan, heat broth, wine and saffron to a gentle simmer.

In a large saucepan, saute onion in a generous amount of olive oil until soft and transparent.

Add the crushed garlic and cook for one minute longer.

Add rice and cook over medium heat for a few minutes, stirring constantly with a wooden spoon allowing the rice to toast a little.

Add 1 cup of broth to the rice mixture, stirring constantly until the rice absorbs the broth.

Turn the heat down to low and add 1/2 cup (2 laddlesful) of stock and continue to cook stirring constantly.

Continue to stir and add more stock as it is absorbed until the rice is tender but firm, about 15 minutes.

Add salt and pepper.

Then stir in the grated parmesan cheese and chopped parsley and serve immediately.


*The saffron gives the rice a beautiful yellow-orange hue and a certain bitterness.
